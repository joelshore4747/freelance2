01 · The brief

A small product range. A loyal audience. And a checkout that wasn't pulling its weight.

Roma had built a quiet little brand on Instagram — three SKUs, regular sell-outs, a real audience. But the existing Shopify site was a stock theme, the subscription option was buried, and "where do I manage my plan?" was a daily DM.

They came to us with two asks: make it feel like a real boutique brand on the web, and make the subscription business actually work — without changing the product, the voice, or the cost of acquisition.

02 · What we did

Editorial product pages. Real subscription portal. The follow-up nobody else was doing.

Three workstreams, six weeks, one launch.

  • Storefront redesign. Editorial product pages with founder voice, ingredient deep-dives, reviews and a one-page checkout that respects mobile-first traffic.
  • Subscription portal. Real account UI — pause, swap, skip — instead of a Shopify default modal. Built on Recharge with custom front-end.
  • Lifecycle email. Welcome series, post-purchase, browse abandon, win-back and a quiet churn-saver in Roma's voice — not the agency-template kind.
  • Hidden plumbing. n8n flow pushes new subscribers to a Notion CRM, alerts the founder on first £100+ order, and auto-tags VIPs at 6 months. None of which the customer ever sees.

03 · Outcomes

The before-and-after that mattered.

Within 90 days of launch, active subscribers more than doubled — without changing media spend. The store's LCP dropped by 1.4 seconds, which Roma's analytics suggested was responsible for roughly a third of the conversion lift on its own.

The churn-saver email — a single send, three days before a planned cancellation — recovered about 14% of would-be churners. Not a miracle. Just the kind of detail nobody had bothered to set up before.

Six months on, the site still ships in 4–8 weeks of small content updates from the team. We've touched it twice. That, too, was part of the point.
